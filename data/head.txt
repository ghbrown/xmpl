
output beginning (head) of file

-- Example 1 --
show first 10 lines (the default number) of foo.txt
  head foo.txt

-- Example 2 --
show first 20 lines of foo.txt, using -n to specify number of lines
  head -n 20 foo.txt

-- Example 3 --
show the first 10 lines of all .txt files in the current directory,
giving each section a header
  head *.txt
